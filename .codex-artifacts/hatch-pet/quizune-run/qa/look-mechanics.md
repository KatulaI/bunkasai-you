Quizune look mechanics

- Natural motion: Quizune is a soft but structured fox mascot. The eyes lead the gaze first, then the muzzle and head turn, then the neck scarf and satchel lag slightly with small follow-through. The feet and lower torso stay broadly anchored so the pet reads as looking around rather than spinning.
- Anchor: Keep the lower body and feet planted to one shared baseline. Preserve practical body scale, belly size, tail volume, and satchel size across all directions.
- Eyes and face: The amber eyes should rotate naturally with clear pupil placement. Eyelids and brow shape can assist the direction. The muzzle and nose tip should shift with head turn so left/right cardinals are unmistakable at pet size.
- Ears: Ears stay attached to the skull and subtly follow head rotation. The nearer ear may appear slightly larger; the farther ear may be slightly occluded in side directions.
- Scarf: The navy scarf is worn and body-attached. It follows the neck/head turn with a small soft lag, never floating separately.
- Satchel: The satchel remains attached across the torso strap and follows the torso. It may swing subtly, but should not jump sides independently or become a new prop cue.

Cardinal pose families

- 000 up: head tipped upward, pupils lifted, muzzle slightly raised, torso still mostly front-facing, tail and satchel stable.
- 090 screen-right: clear rightward head turn in viewer coordinates, nose and pupils shifted toward screen-right, right side of muzzle more prominent, left side slightly occluded.
- 180 down: head dipped downward, pupils lowered, eyelids slightly compressed, upper body leaning forward just a little.
- 270 screen-left: clear leftward head turn in viewer coordinates, nose and pupils shifted toward screen-left, left side of muzzle more prominent, right side slightly occluded.

Interpolation rules

- Move through directions as one smooth clockwise family in even 22.5-degree steps.
- Use diagonals as blended combinations of the adjacent cardinals, not separate inventions.
- Preserve identity: same face shape, tail shape, satchel strap path, scarf knot, and overall silhouette family.
- Avoid whole-sprite rotation, tilt-only cheating, or detached prop drift.
